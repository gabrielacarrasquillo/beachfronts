The largest new hotel developments in Puerto Rico are beachfronts established on the north coast

Building permits for inns on the island soared in the aftermath of Hurricane Maria, and those located in the land maritime zone were found to be driven by foreign investment.

By Gabriela Carrasquillo Piñeiro

With the idea of living in paradise, several developers are pushing new projects to build luxury beachfront lodges in the municipalities of Dorado and Rio Grande in Puerto Rico. This is the conclusion of an analysis of the database of permits issued by the Office of Management and Permits (OGPe, in Spanish), which manages these procedures.

With a focused look at permits for the construction of hotels, condohotels and tourist lodges in the past 10 years (from 2012 to 2021), it was found that the number of permits submitted and approved increased after Hurricane Maria. Similarly, the data show that between 2012 and 2014, this type of development was uncommon or non-existent. After filtering the OGPe's database, project descriptions were evaluated and assigned one of the following categories: Developments and Expansion/Renewal. This is to distinguish the impacts of building permits.

Considering that development projects have a greater impact on the ground than those of renewal and expansion, the analysis focused on those assigned the Development category to see how close to the coast these projects are of greater impact. Using the Google Earth tool, the locations of all the projects were visited and it was found that three developments in the coastal area of the island were highlighted with more than one project in the area under the same name and with several permits issued. The rest of the permits appear to be local projects interned in coastal villages and not in front of the beach.

It was found that there were several projects that are on the same site, so the search was made for who are the managers of these projects, what's their provenance and how they relate to these efforts.
